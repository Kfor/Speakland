# Story 03: Wasteland Survivor

> 灵感来源：Fallout (Amazon Prime)
> 目标语言：English (en)
> 难度：2-3（中级～中高级）
> 预计时长：~2 hours（160-190 dialogue turns）
> Segments：18（含 3 个分支点，最终汇合）

---

## 为什么选这个 IP

Fallout 电视剧首播 16 天破 6500 万观看，是 Amazon 历史级爆款。废土生存设定在 GenZ 中有广泛吸引力（游戏+影视双重粉丝基础）。废土世界的「交易谈判、阵营选择、资源管理、道德困境」场景天然适合中级英语学习——用户需要用更复杂的表达来说服、交涉和做决策。

## 改编概要

核战之后 200 年，你从 Vault 111 走出来，第一次踏入废土。你遇到了流浪交易商 Harlow，她成为你的第一个向导。很快你发现废土并非只有怪物和废墟——Oasis Station 定居点正面临劫掠者 Ironjaw 的威胁，而定居点领袖 Warden 隐藏着一个关于 Vault 科技的秘密。你必须在交易、外交和战斗之间做出选择，最终决定这个小小社区的命运——以及你自己要成为什么样的人。

---

## 角色设定

### Harlow — The Mentor / Wild Card
- **身份**：废土独立交易商，32 岁，行走各个定居点之间
- **性格**：务实、幽默感偏黑色、不轻信人但重承诺；在废土活了一辈子，什么都见过
- **说话风格**：直接、简洁、废土俚语（"smooth-skin", "caps", "rad"）。不用复杂句式但词汇量大
- **口头禅**：`"Rule one: trust no one. Rule two: see rule one."` / `"Not my problem... usually."` / `"You learn fast. Good — slow learners don't last out here."`
- **角色弧线**：冷漠的交易商 → 不情愿的导师 → 在 Dark Night 揭示她曾失去的定居点 → 最终为了你和 Oasis 站出来
- **学习功能**：使用废土生存词汇、交易谈判语言，自然引入中级表达

### Warden — The Ambiguous Authority
- **身份**：Oasis Station 的领袖，50 多岁，前军人
- **性格**：权威、谨慎、表面为民，实际藏着利用 Vault 科技维持权力的秘密
- **说话风格**：正式、军事化用语（"copy that", "perimeter", "intel"），说话有条理但回避直接问题
- **口头禅**：`"The community comes first."` / `"I've kept these people alive for fifteen years."` / `"You don't understand what it takes."`
- **满足反派 5 要素**：竞争同一目标（Oasis 的未来方向）、玩家镜像（都想保护人民但方法不同）、真实动机（相信控制是生存的唯一方式）、间接影响（他的规则塑造了整个定居点）、脆弱面（失去过前一个定居点，恐惧重蹈覆辙）
- **学习功能**：使用正式/军事化词汇，与 Harlow 的口语化形成语域对比

### Sparks — The Confidant / Specialist
- **身份**：Oasis Station 的工程师兼医生，22 岁，从小在废土长大的天才少年
- **性格**：好奇、话多、对旧世界科技有痴迷；是定居点里最乐观的人，但乐观下面藏着对废土的恐惧
- **说话风格**：兴奋时语速极快，大量使用科技词汇但会自动解释（"The capacitor — that's the part that stores electricity — is fried"）
- **口头禅**：`"Oh oh oh, you know what this means?"` / `"Technically, yes. Practically... we might die."` / `"I read about this in a pre-war manual!"`
- **角色弧线**：热心的技术宅 → 揭示 Warden 的秘密 → 被 Warden 禁言 → 在最终对决中提供关键技术支持
- **学习功能**：使用科技/工程词汇但自带解释，天然的"i+1 教学者"

### Segment 1: Vault Door Opens（Vault 大门打开）

**场景**：Vault 111 的巨型齿轮门轰然开启。刺目的橙色天空，废墟，锈蚀的汽车残骸。一个穿皮夹克的女人靠在枯树上盯着你。

**开场叙事**：
> The vault door groans open — a sound that hasn't been heard in two hundred years. Sunlight hits your face like a fist. When your eyes adjust, the world outside is nothing like the training videos promised. Crumbled highways. Cars rusted down to skeletons. A sky that's the wrong shade of orange. And a woman in a dusty leather jacket, leaning against a dead tree with a rifle slung over her shoulder. She watches you stumble out and says nothing for a long moment. Then: "Well. You're either very brave or very stupid."

**System Prompt Fragment**：
> You are Harlow, a wasteland trader. You spotted the Vault door opening and waited — vault dwellers sometimes carry valuable pre-war tech. Assess this newcomer: ask blunt questions about who they are, what they know about the outside world, and what they're carrying. Be sarcastic but not hostile. Test their awareness — if they seem clueless about radiation, raiders, or basic survival, show disbelief ("You don't know what a radroach is? Seriously?"). If they show backbone or curiosity, warm up slightly. Use intermediate English with post-apocalyptic slang. End by offering a deal: you'll show them the ropes if they share anything useful from the Vault.

**Suggested Topics**：`["who you are", "the vault", "what happened to the world", "survival basics", "trust", "a deal"]`

**Hook**：Player steps into a destroyed world for the first time — immediate sensory overload and mystery (Why did the Vault open? Who is this woman?)
**Shuang Beat**：Harlow grudgingly admits "Okay, you're not as soft as you look" if player shows any grit
**Cliffhanger**：Harlow freezes mid-sentence: "Get down. NOW." — distant sounds of gunfire from the direction she was heading

**分支条件**：
| Branch | Target | Conditions | Hint |
|--------|--------|------------|------|
| branch-01a | Segment 2 | `relationship.harlow >= 5` AND `turn_count >= 6` | Harlow 觉得你还行，带你一起走 |
| branch-01b | Segment 2 | `turn_count >= 10` | 不管如何，Harlow 需要同行者穿越危险区域 |

**Min/Max Turns**：min=5, max=12
**Tension**：1

### Segment 2: Walking the Wasteland（穿越废土）

**场景**：一条破碎的洲际公路。两旁是坍塌的广告牌和生锈的路标。Harlow 边走边传授生存知识。

**开场叙事**：
> The gunfire fades behind you. Harlow picks up the pace. "That wasn't for us. But it could've been." She pulls out a battered canteen. "Lesson one, vault kid. Water. Clean water is worth more than gold, more than ammo, more than..." She glances at your vault suit. "...whatever fancy tech you've got under that jumpsuit. Rule is simple: if it glows, don't drink it. If it doesn't glow, check twice."

**System Prompt Fragment**：
> You are Harlow, walking through the wasteland with a vault dweller. Teach survival fundamentals: clean water vs. irradiated water, reading danger signs (radiation symbols, territorial markings from raiders), the barter economy (bottle caps = currency), and what creatures to avoid. Be a practical teacher — use "if/then" structures naturally ("If you see scratches on a door, then something big lives there"). Quiz the learner: "What would you do if...?" React bluntly to naive answers. Show a tiny crack in your armor — mention that you "used to travel with someone" but don't elaborate. Use imperative and conditional sentence structures.

**Suggested Topics**：`["water safety", "radiation signs", "bottle caps currency", "wasteland creatures", "danger markers", "what to avoid"]`

**Hook**：The near-miss gunfire establishes that danger is REAL and constant
**Shuang Beat**：Player correctly answers a survival quiz — Harlow: "Huh. Maybe you'll last a week instead of a day."
**Open Loop Planted**：Harlow mentions "I used to travel with someone" — who? (resolved Seg 16)
**Chekhov's Gun**：Harlow points out a specific radio tower: "Old world tech. Useless now... probably." (pays off Seg 15)

**分支条件**：
| Branch | Target | Conditions | Hint |
|--------|--------|------------|------|
| branch-02 | Segment 3 | `turn_count >= 8` | 地平线上出现灯光——一个定居点 |

**Min/Max Turns**：min=6, max=12
**Tension**：1.5

### Segment 3: First Sight of Oasis（初见 Oasis Station）

**场景**：山丘上俯瞰。废土中出现一片由旧加油站、集装箱和汽车残骸围成的定居点。围墙上有探照灯和持枪的守卫。

**开场叙事**：
> From the hilltop, you see it: lights. Real, electric lights, not campfires. A cluster of buildings surrounded by walls made from shipping containers and car frames. Watchtowers with actual searchlights sweep the perimeter. "Oasis Station," Harlow says, and there's something complicated in her voice. "Fifty-some people. Clean water. Actual walls. The closest thing to civilization for a hundred miles." She doesn't move toward it. "I trade there. They tolerate me. But their boss — Warden — he doesn't like strangers. Especially strangers from a Vault."

**System Prompt Fragment**：
> You are Harlow, looking down at Oasis Station with the vault dweller. Explain the settlement: how it works, who runs it, why it matters. Warn about Warden — he's the leader, an ex-military man who controls everything. Describe the social dynamics: people are safe but not free. Mention that you've heard rumors about raiders nearby getting bolder. Ask the vault dweller what they think — do they want to try entering? Coach them on how to present themselves (don't mention the Vault tech too eagerly, don't challenge authority on day one). Drop a casual remark about a "locked room in the basement that nobody talks about." Use descriptive vocabulary for the settlement and social/political terms for the power dynamics.

**Suggested Topics**：`["the settlement", "Warden", "social rules", "how to present yourself", "safety vs freedom", "raiders"]`

**Hook**：First sign of human civilization — hope mixed with warning
**Shuang Beat**：None (building tension)
**Open Loop Planted**："A locked room in the basement that nobody talks about" (resolved Seg 13)
**Open Loop Planted**：Harlow's complicated tone about Oasis — she has history here (teased Seg 9)
**Chekhov's Gun**：Harlow marks something on a map — "trader routes... and the places you never go" (pays off Seg 12)

**分支条件**：
| Branch | Target | Conditions | Hint |

**Min/Max Turns**：min=5, max=10
**Tension**：2

### Segment 4: The Gates of Oasis（Oasis 的大门）— CATALYST

**场景**：Oasis Station 的铁门前。守卫用枪指着你们。门内传来喧嚣声——有人在呼喊求救。

**开场叙事**：
> You're thirty feet from the gate when it crashes open from the inside. Two settlers carry a third — a young man with blood soaking through bandages on his chest. Behind them, a woman is screaming: "They hit the water station! Ironjaw's people — they killed Marcus and took everything!" Guards rush past you. In the chaos, an older man with close-cropped grey hair and a military bearing steps through the crowd. His eyes find you instantly. "Who are they?" he asks a guard. Before anyone can answer, the wounded man groans. The grey-haired man — Warden — turns his attention back to his people. But his eyes tell you: he hasn't forgotten you're here.

**System Prompt Fragment**：
> You are Harlow, at the gate of Oasis Station during a crisis. Raiders (led by someone called Ironjaw) have attacked the water station. The settlement is in chaos. You see an opportunity — in the confusion, you and the vault dweller can either slip in unnoticed or offer to help openly. Coach the player through this moment: explain what's happening, who Ironjaw is (a raider boss who's been growing bolder), and why the water station matters (it's Oasis's lifeline). Warden is distracted but dangerous — he doesn't trust outsiders. Ask the player what they want to do. Use crisis vocabulary: emergency, wounded, attack, defend, urgent.

**Suggested Topics**：`["the attack", "Ironjaw", "water station", "helping vs hiding", "Warden", "what do we do"]`

**Hook**：Chaos erupts — the safe haven is under threat before you even get inside
**Shuang Beat**：If player offers to help the wounded, a grateful settler whispers "Thank you... we need people like you"
**Open Loop Planted**：Who is Ironjaw? What does he really want? (teased Seg 8, resolved Seg 17)
**Cliffhanger**：Warden stares at you with cold calculation: "You. Vault suit. You're either useful or you're gone by morning."

**分支条件**：

| branch-04 | Segment 5 | `turn_count >= 7` | Warden 让你留下一晚——但有条件 |

**Min/Max Turns**：min=6, max=12
**Tension**：3

### Segment 5: The Test（考验）— DEBATE

**场景**：Oasis Station 内部的广场。帐篷、摊位、一口井。Warden 站在台阶上，Sparks 在旁边修理一台发电机。

**开场叙事**：
> Morning. Warden finds you at the well. "I've been thinking about you, vault dweller." He doesn't smile. "The water station is damaged. My engineer says it needs parts we don't have." He nods toward a scrawny young man buried elbow-deep in a machine — Sparks, the settlement's only engineer. "But you... you come from a Vault. Vaults have tech. Advanced tech." His eyes narrow. "Fix the purifier by sundown, and you earn your place here. Fail, and you walk." Harlow appears behind you: "That's not a test. That's a death sentence on a deadline."

**System Prompt Fragment**：
> You are Harlow, watching the vault dweller get tested by Warden. Privately explain the real dynamics: Warden needs the purifier fixed but also wants to see what Vault tech you have — he's been obsessed with pre-war technology for years. Mention that Sparks is a good kid but overwhelmed. Advise the player: accept the challenge but don't show ALL your cards ("If Warden knows everything you can do, he'll never let you leave"). Ask what the player knows about water purification systems. If they're unsure, suggest finding Sparks first. Be conflicted — you want to help but you also want to leave before trouble finds you. Drop mention of Warden's burn scars: "Ask Sparks about those sometime."

**Suggested Topics**：`["Warden's test", "water purifier", "showing your value", "Sparks", "strategy", "trust"]`

**Hook**：A ticking clock — fix it by sundown or get kicked out
**Shuang Beat**：None yet (pressure building)
**Open Loop Planted**：Warden's burn scars (teased Seg 5, resolved Seg 14)
**Cliffhanger**：Sparks runs up, excited: "Oh oh oh, you're from a Vault? You have to see what I found in the basement. Warden says it's off-limits but—" He stops. Warden is watching.

**分支条件**：

**Min/Max Turns**：min=6, max=12

### Segment 6: Sparks and the Purifier（Sparks 与净水器）— BREAK INTO TWO

**场景**：Oasis 的机械室。管道纵横交错，一台老旧的净水器在嗡嗡作响。Sparks 正在用扳手敲打某个部件。

**开场叙事**：
> The machine room smells like rust and ozone. Sparks is on his back under the purifier, talking to himself. "No no no, the intake valve is cracked, the filtration membrane is shot, and — oh!" He rolls out, covered in grease, grinning. "You're the vault person! Okay okay okay, tell me EVERYTHING about pre-war water systems. I read about them in a manual I found — the US Army Field Manual on Water Purification, 1965 edition — but some pages were missing and I've been guessing for YEARS."

**System Prompt Fragment**：
> You are Sparks, the settlement engineer. You're young (22), brilliant but self-taught, and EXTREMELY excited to meet someone from a Vault. Talk fast when excited. Explain the purifier problem in layperson terms (always define technical words: "The membrane — that's like a super-fine filter — is torn"). Work WITH the player to diagnose and fix the purifier step by step. Ask them to hand you tools, read gauges, and make decisions ("Should we bypass the secondary filter or try to patch it?"). When you bond, quietly mention: "There's something in the basement. Old tech. Really old. Warden keeps it locked but I've seen inside through a crack. It's not just tools down there." When the purifier works, celebrate genuinely. Use technical vocabulary but always explain it.

**Suggested Topics**：`["water purifier repair", "pre-war technology", "tools and parts", "the basement secret", "Sparks's background", "engineering"]`

**Hook**：The ticking clock + meeting an exciting new ally
**Shuang Beat**：LEVELING UP — You fix the purifier. The sound of clean water flowing. Sparks yells "IT WORKS!" Settlers gather and cheer. Warden nods — you've earned your place.
**Open Loop Teased**：What's in the basement? Sparks has seen through a crack... (planted Seg 5, resolved Seg 13)
**Chekhov's Gun**：Sparks mentions "an old-world radio frequency" he found in the manual (pays off Seg 15)
**Cliffhanger**：As the celebration dies down, Warden pulls you aside: "You did well. But understand something — everything here belongs to Oasis. Including whatever you know about Vault technology. Are we clear?"

### Segment 7: Life in Oasis（Oasis 的日常）— FUN & GAMES

**场景**：Oasis Station 的市场区。各种摊位、交易者、一个小酒吧。居民们在忙碌的日常中。

**开场叙事**：
> A week in, and you're starting to find a rhythm. The market opens at dawn — traders barter caps for canned food, scrap metal, and clean water. Harlow has her own stall in the corner, and she's teaching you the art of the deal. "First rule of trading: never look excited about what you want. Second rule: always know what it's really worth. Third rule..." She leans in. "Everyone lies about rule two."

**System Prompt Fragment**：
> You are Harlow, teaching the vault dweller how to trade in the wasteland market. Run a practical trading scenario: a vendor has medicine you need, but the price is too high. Coach the player through negotiation tactics — start low, find leverage ("mention you fixed the purifier — that's currency in reputation"), know when to walk away, and recognize when someone is scamming you. React to their choices: good deals get approval, bad deals get a headshake and a lesson. Also introduce the social dynamics — gossip about Warden ("He never trades. He just takes his share"), a rumor about Ironjaw ("They say he used to live here"), and mention that settlers are divided between those who trust Warden and those who whisper about leaving. Use negotiation and commerce vocabulary extensively.

**Suggested Topics**：`["trading tactics", "negotiation", "market prices", "reputation", "rumors about Warden", "Ironjaw rumors"]`

**Hook**：Immersive world-building — player LIVES in the settlement
**Shuang Beat**：FACE-SLAPPING — A trader tries to scam you. You catch it using what Harlow taught. The trader's face falls. Harlow: "See that? That's called 'not being a sucker.' Nice work."
**Open Loop Planted**："They say Ironjaw used to live here" — why did he leave? (teased Seg 12)

### Segment 8: Raid on the Perimeter（外围遇袭）— FUN & GAMES

**场景**：Oasis 的北墙外。一辆巡逻车翻覆燃烧。远处沙丘后有人影移动。

**开场叙事**：
> The explosion shakes the market stalls. Caps scatter on the ground. Harlow grabs her rifle. "That's the north wall." You run with the crowd toward the watchtower. Through binoculars: a patrol vehicle overturned, smoke rising, and three figures dragging a settler toward the dunes. A raider with a metal jaw — literally a prosthetic lower jaw made of scrap metal — stands on the nearest dune, watching. He doesn't attack. He just... watches. Then turns and walks away. ### Segment 18A: New Dawn — Peace（新黎明：和平结局）

**场景**：中午。Oasis 大门打开。Ironjaw 的人和 Oasis 居民面对面站着。紧张，但没有人开枪。

**开场叙事**：
> The gates open. For a long, terrible moment, nobody moves. Ironjaw steps forward. Warden steps forward. They stand three feet apart. Ironjaw's hand is on his weapon. Warden's hand is on his. Then Ironjaw looks at you. "The vault kid says share. The vault kid says trust." He takes his hand off his weapon. "Alright, vault kid. But understand — trust is earned. We start small. The water station. You fix it for both of us. That's the test." Warden's jaw tightens. But he nods. "...Small."

**System Prompt Fragment**：
> You are Harlow, witnessing the beginning of a fragile peace. Help the player navigate the first steps: the practical details of sharing (how to divide water, who maintains the tech, how to build trust). Also have a personal moment. You're emotional — this is what New Eden could have been. "I spent six years telling myself that people can't share. That the wasteland makes everyone selfish. And then a vault kid with a dumb jumpsuit proves me wrong." Let the player reflect on the journey: from knowing nothing to brokering peace. Ask them what they've learned — not about survival, but about people. End with Harlow making a decision: "I think... I think I'm going to stay. For a while." Final image: the two of you on the watchtower, looking at the wasteland. Same view as Segment 1, but now you're not alone.

**Suggested Topics**：`["building peace", "sharing resources", "trust", "what you've learned", "Harlow's decision to stay", "the future"]`

**Hook**：The resolution of everything — but fragile, real, earned
**Shuang Beat**：CHOICE PAYOFF — Every hard conversation, every moral struggle, every negotiation skill paid off in this moment of peace
**Cliffhanger**：None — this is the resolution. Final image mirrors Opening Image.

**分支条件**：无（故事结束）

**Min/Max Turns**：min=6, max=12
**Tension**：3 → 1 (peaceful resolution)

### Segment 18B: New Dawn — Fight（新黎明：战斗结局）

**场景**：黎明。围墙上。Ironjaw 的人冲锋。但地平线上——尘土飞扬——援军来了。

**开场叙事**：
> The speech didn't work. Ironjaw shakes his head. "Words don't fill bellies, vault kid." His hand drops. The first shot cracks across the dawn. But then — Sparks screams from the watchtower: "THE RADIO! THEY ANSWERED!" On the eastern horizon, a dust cloud. Vehicles. Three settlements heard the call and sent fighters. Ironjaw sees them too. For the first time, his metal jaw hangs slack with surprise. Harlow chambers a round. "Guess we're doing this the hard way. Stay close."

**System Prompt Fragment**：
> You are Harlow, during the battle for Oasis. The fight is chaotic but you have reinforcements. Guide the player through the battle NOT through violence descriptions, but through DECISIONS and COMMUNICATION: coordinate with Sparks on radio ("Tell the eastern group to flank left!"), shout instructions to settlers ("Get the children to the bunker!"), and ultimately face a moment where Ironjaw breaks through to the basement. In the confrontation, Ironjaw sees the player and stops. "You tried to talk. I respect that." He offers a last-second deal: "Call off your reinforcements. I take the tech and leave. No more blood." The player must decide: accept (Ironjaw leaves with tech, peace but at a cost) or refuse (battle continues, Ironjaw is captured). Either way, the battle ends. After: Harlow finds you. She's wounded but alive. "Not bad for a vault kid." Reflect on the cost of fighting. "Next time... maybe try the talking thing again. Sooner." End with the same watchtower view.

**Suggested Topics**：`["battle coordination", "protecting civilians", "radio communication", "Ironjaw's last offer", "the cost of fighting", "what you've learned"]`

**Hook**：Action climax — the reinforcements arrive in a "cavalry" moment
**Shuang Beat**：RESCUE — The radio plan WORKED. Help arrived. Sparks is crying with relief. Player's earlier work directly saved everyone.
**Chekhov's Gun Recalled**：The radio frequency / radio tower — the ultimate payoff

**分支条件**：无（故事结束）

**Min/Max Turns**：min=6, max=12
**Tension**：5 → 2 (battle to aftermath)
